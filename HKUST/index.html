About this Conference

Venue: Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST)

Tentative Date: August 2018 (3-day conference)                                                                                                                                                                          

This conference will be held at The Geotechnical Centrifuge Facility at Hong Kong University of Science and Technology. In there we have the World's first 1st 2D Hydraulic In Flight Shaking Table and the The World's 2nd 4-Axis Robotic Manipulator

Public Transportation

  • MTR Transit

    The Mass Transit Railway (MTR) is the rapid transit railway system in Hong Kong. It is one of the most profitable systems in the world.  When coming to HKUST, please get off at the Hang Hau Station and take either a taxi or one of the following minibuses stated below.

  • MINIBUS

    From the MTR station at Hang Hau, take exist B2 to reach the terminal exchange. Taking minibus 11m will take you directly to HKUST's North Entrance. SImilarly, minibus 11 will take you directly to HKUST's South Entrance, which is where our Geotechnical Centrifuge is located.

  • BUS

    Taking 91M from the terminal exchange from the Hang Hau station will bring you to a gas station located outside of HKUST's South Entrance. From there, walk through the street,  past the designer studio to reach the South Entrance of HKUST.



Geo Energy Themes:
                                                                                                                                                                                    Geo Environment Themes:

*Energy geo-structures such as piles, walls and tunnels

*Geo-storages such as CO2

*Energy extraction applications such as conventional and unconventional energy,
and geothermal energy

*Energy policy and management: geotechnical aspects sequestration and radioactive waste disposal


*Reused and recycled materials for geotechnical works

*Bio-geotechnology such as the use of vegetation and bacteria

*Landfills

*Geo-environmental policy and management
